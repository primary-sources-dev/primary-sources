Lee Harvey Oswald

Alek James Hidell O.H. Lee
Born  Oct 18, 1939 Died  Nov 24, 1963 Place  New Orleans, LA Age  24 Nation  American Cause  Gunshot (Jack Ruby)

Biographical Summary

Former U.S. Marine who defected to the Soviet Union in 1959 and lived in Minsk until 1962. Married to Soviet national Marina Prusakova. Returned to the United States in June 1962. Active in New Orleans with the Fair Play for Cuba Committee. Employed at the Texas School Book Depository in Dallas, Texas from October 1963. Accused of assassinating President John F. Kennedy on November 22, 1963. Denied all charges. Shot and killed by nightclub owner Jack Ruby on November 24, 1963 in Dallas Police headquarters.

The Warren Commission (1964) concluded he was the sole assassin. He remains the only named suspect in the assassination. No criminal conviction was ever obtained.

Key Dates

Sep 1959

Received hardship discharge from USMC.

Oct 1959

Defected to Soviet Union. Attempted to renounce U.S. citizenship at Moscow embassy.

Apr 1961

Married Marina Nikolayevna Prusakova in Minsk, Byelorussian SSR.

Jun 1962

Returned to the United States with Marina and daughter June.

Apr 1963

Alleged shooting attempt on General Edwin Walker in Dallas. No conviction.

Aug 1963

Distributed Fair Play for Cuba Committee leaflets in New Orleans. Arrested in street altercation.

Oct 16, 1963

Began employment at Texas School Book Depository, Dallas.

Nov 22, 1963

12:30 PM — JFK assassinated in Dealey Plaza. Dallas Police Officer J.D. Tippit shot and killed. Oswald arrested at Texas Theatre, Oak Cliff.

Nov 24, 1963

Shot by Jack Ruby at Dallas Police HQ during transfer. Died 1:07 PM at Parkland Memorial Hospital.
